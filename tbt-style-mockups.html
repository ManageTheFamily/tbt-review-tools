Design Notes

Lora carries the editorial authority for titles and names. Poppins handles all functional text: stats, labels, captions. The pairing creates a "documentary overlay on a game" feel rather than a "game UI" feel. The gold accent ties everything together without becoming dominant.

Chapter Title Cards
Full-screen cards shown for 2 to 3 seconds between chapters. These give the viewer a breathing point and signal narrative structure. Click through different chapters to see how the template handles varying title lengths and tonal accents.

Design Notes

  • Full-screen on deep navy, centered. Clean and deliberate. No game imagery visible during the card.
  • Gold horizontal rule below the title provides visual anchor without being loud.
  • Chapter number in Poppins uppercase with letter-spacing reads as a functional label, not decoration.
  • Title in Lora serif at display size. Even the longest title (Ch 10) fits comfortably at this scale.
  • Fade in over ~0.8s, hold 2s, fade out. No slide or zoom. Documentary pacing.

Style Comparison

  • Style A (Bar Caption): Clean Poppins on dark bar with gold border. Most "documentary" feeling. Treats the joke with visual seriousness, which makes it funnier.
  • Style B (Impact Text): Bold white text with black outline. Classic meme/YouTube style. Higher energy, less documentary.
  • Style C (Documentary): Italic Lora with gold left-border. Treats the caption like a documentary quote. Driest delivery of the three.
  • Recommendation: Style A or C for consistency with the documentary tone. Style B for moments that need extra comedic punch.

Design Notes

  • Typography-first approach. "TYRANNY" in warm gold at display size is the visual anchor. "TURN-BASED" in cream above it at smaller weight.
  • Subtitle in Poppins light weight with generous letter-spacing reads as a tagline, not a title.
  • Gold rule divider echoes the chapter card treatment. Creates visual continuity.
  • Deep navy background with subtle radial gold glow. No imagery, no icons. The words do the work.
  • This is a starting point. Could add a graphic element (hex grid fragment, chess piece silhouette, broken column) if typography alone feels too spare.
