Sharing Your Gameplay Videos with Seth

How to upload your raw recordings to Google Drive

Seth needs your 4 gameplay recordings from Game 1 as actual video files. Google Drive preserves the original quality exactly as recorded, which is why we use it instead of YouTube for file transfer.

What you need
Your 4 Civ VII gameplay recordings and a Google account. The whole process takes about 10 minutes of active work, plus upload time (which runs in the background).

Step 1: Find Your Recordings

  1. Open the folder on your computer where your recording software saves videos.
  2. If you're not sure where that is:
    • OBS: Open OBS, click Settings, click Output, look at Recording Path
    • ShadowPlay / GeForce Experience: Open GeForce Experience, click the gear icon, click IN-GAME OVERLAY, click Settings, look for the save location
    • Still can't find them? Open File Explorer, type *.mp4 in the search bar (top right), sort by Date, look for large files from when you recorded
  3. You should have 4 video files. The sizes should be roughly:
Tape Duration Expected Size
Tape 1 (first session) ~1 hour 3 – 6 GB
Tape 2 ~2 hours 6 – 12 GB
Tape 3 ~2 hours 6 – 12 GB
Tape 4 (short one) ~21 minutes 1 – 2 GB

Step 2: Create a Folder in Google Drive

  1. Open your web browser and go to drive.google.com
  2. Click the + New button in the top left corner
  3. Click New folder
  4. Name it: TBT Raw Gameplay
  5. Click Create, then double-click the new folder to open it

Step 3: Upload the Files

  1. Drag all 4 video files from your computer into the Google Drive browser window
  2. You'll see a progress bar in the bottom right corner
  3. Wait for all uploads to finish. This could take a few hours depending on your internet speed. Don't close the browser until every file says "Upload complete."
Important
Keep the browser tab open while uploading. You can use other tabs and do other things on your computer. Just don't close the Drive tab or shut down your computer until it's done.

Step 4: Share the Folder with Seth

  1. Go back to your main Google Drive (click My Drive on the left)
  2. Right-click the TBT Raw Gameplay folder
  3. Click Share
  4. Type Seth's email address
  5. Click Send

Step 5: Tell Seth Which Video Is Which

Text or message Seth with a quick list so he knows which file is which tape. Something like:

"Here are the 4 files:

[filename 1] — first recording session (~1 hour)
[filename 2] — second session (~2 hours)
[filename 3] — third session (~2 hours)
[filename 4] — last session (~21 minutes, the short one)"

Replace [filename] with whatever your files are actually called. If you're not sure about the order, just list the filenames and how long each one is. Seth can figure it out from there.

✓
That's it!
Let Seth know once the uploads are done.
